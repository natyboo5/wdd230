City Hall

Port St. Lucie became a city on April 27, 1961. The first official commision meeting was held in May 9, 1961 where W.C. Farmer was elected as the first Mayor.

Demographics

Port St. Lucie has a total population of about 120.000 habitants, with a population density of 1,525/sq mile. There is almost 50,000 households with an avergae household income of $63,300. There are more than 4,360 businesses in the area and the travel time ranges from 15 to 29 minutes.

Town of Tradition

The unique masterplanned community of Tradition captures the charm of America's small towns of yesterday and combines that with the amenities today's home buyers expect. The Town also contains the Tradition Center for Innovation which is the home of Torrey Pines Institute, Memorial Hospital and Mann Research Center.

Port St. Lucie Botanical Gardens

The Port St. Lucie Botanical Gardens is a welcome oasis of greenery in the heart ofthe city. The Gardens is located on 20 acres of property on the banks of the North Fork of the St. Lucie River.

Entertainment

The city is home to New York Mets spring training, the St. Lucie Mets Florida State League team and the Mets rookie level team in the Gulf Coast League. All three play at Clover Park. The Treasure Coast Galleons, a semi-pro soccer team also calls Port St. Lucie its home. .
